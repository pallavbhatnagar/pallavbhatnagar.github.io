Bonds Online

Wireframe to Visual Design

Worked very briefly on the Indiabonds website, I was responsible for designing their Landing pages. Through a series of incremental optimization as the website scales to more challenges. A cyclic process of prototyping, testing, analyzing, and refining at each stage, facing the challenges and solving them one by one.



Getting Context

All online experiences should end in action, and landing pages are the moment the excitement your product generated crystallizes into a leap of faith. From simple ‘buy now’ buttons to more complex experiences, landing pages represent a commitment or decision from the visitor.

Indiabonds is an online portal selling bonds onfline. A financial company driven by technology with a passion to create an investment platform that provides access to the fixed income market in a low-cost, transparent and easy to use manner.



The Challenge

As a third party UX designer, my agency assigned me for few weeks onto the assignment of designing a full fledged new website for Indiabonds. I started with the Landing page as it is where people “land” first. They are excited and curious, but are they willing to take the next step? This consideration makes landing page design a top priority when designing any kind of campaign or online experience. Sometimes it takes a little experimentation to get it right.

Motivating users to click, buy a product, fill out a form, or any other action means creating trust and confidence. The choice or commitment the user is making and the reward they get should be clear, and they should be able to take the action easily, on any screen or environment.

The success of the page depends entirely on people taking action, which will drive the strategy. Each landing page has its own purpose, function and audience, which constrains your design, depending on what the landing page is used for. These choices call for bursts of creativity but also intuitive design. Here are some best practices in building engines of action.

02 — Final SET of Visual Design

Visual Designs

The creation of visual concepts is a process that many product designers may be familiar with. My process may differ a bit from others, or may be altogether unfamiliar to those who only see the completed concepts. Often I find that people think it simply ‘happens’. I assure you, it does not. The process can cycle through 4 phases: Wireframe creation, Visual Discovery, Digital Execution, & then Presentation. As a Product Designer, I focus on the process I take once wireframes have been created.





This process allowed me to explore a plethora of options as we emerge with the final ones to present.
