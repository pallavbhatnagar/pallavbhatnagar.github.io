About Me

Being a kid in the 1990s was the most exhilarating experience for me, with the up and coming technology and the introduction of it all in India. In a way, we grew with the tech, and my computer became my best friend.

The anticipation and imagination of what this tech behind Computer Science is and what it could be drove me to my graduation in Engineering, but I soon realized that as had been for years, Design was my ultimate calling. I had been designing posters and banners to graphics and basic websites since teenage, as a freelancer but it was during my grad years that my exposure to this world maximized and I could see, clear as day, my path ahead.

An avid comic reader with a love for all things sci-fi, I always believed Design can create a difference and experienced it first-hand during my year as a Creative Visualizer for a national newspaper. During the COVID-19 pandemic, I worked on upgrading my skills in redesigning the Human-Computer Interface and User Experience and am currently striving everyday to ease the complexity of technology through my designs.

Engineer to UX Designer

My stint with Engineering might have been only four years long, but my work in, with and around Computer Science was, is and will always be ongoing. However, alongside my conception of all things computers, I realized that to be an effective problem solver, it is not only crucial to compute & design for user needs but to also comprehend the bigger picture of how a product fits into a company's set of goals, mission, strategies and brand – this made the process of discerning user perspectives as well as the emotions that influence their interaction with digital products not just increasingly enlightening, but also immensely enjoyable, to me as an engineer who turned out to be a product deisgner.
